# Measuring What We’re Missing

## What This Is
This article proposes a framework for measuring the effectiveness of security detection and response by focusing on testing assumptions and quantifying false negatives within specific control areas. It emphasizes tracking improvements over time and differentiating between measured effectiveness and newly discovered gaps.

## Why It Matters
This piece is crucial for application security engineers as it provides a more meaningful way to assess security control effectiveness beyond simple activity metrics, enabling a better understanding of actual risk reduction and areas for improvement in detection capabilities.

## Key Points
- Detection effectiveness can be approximated by measuring detected threats against the sum of detected and missed threats under test conditions.
- False negatives become meaningful when tied to deliberate testing, providing a measurable gap within a defined scope.
- Results should be mapped to existing control areas (e.g., Identity, Endpoint, Network) for better context and aggregation.
- Improvements are tracked by comparing subsequent test results rather than rewriting historical data.
- A clear distinction should be maintained between an 'effectiveness score' (based on tests) and 'discovery counts' (for new, un-tested gaps).
